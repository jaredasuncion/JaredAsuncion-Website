Hello there! My name is Jared Asuncion and I am an aspiring filmmaker that is currently studying film at the University of California, Riverside. Born in West Covina, I moved to Corona, California for elementary school and to adjust to a new lifestyle. Afterwards, I attended junior high and high school at Eastvale while still living in Corona while simultaneously living in Chino after my parents split. Graduating from Eleanor Roosevelt High School as part of the class of 2019, I currently continue my studies at UCR.

As a second-generation Filipino-American, it is uncommon for one to pursue a career in filmmaking. Since I have developed a fascination for movies as far as my childhood, delving into the behind-the-scenes of making films piqued my interests and led to me wanting to study film. My all-time favorite movie is E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial, directed by the great Steven Spielberg. This film held a special place in my heart and was the starting point of my journey of studying film.

Prior to studying film, I would collaborate with friends from high school to film videos for school projects and for fun. My high school class offered a video production course which emphasizes how to record and edit videos, which I was blessed to have taken. The knowledge I have acquired from this class carried over at UCR, where I took a Shakespeare class to understand how Shakespeare's legacy lives on today. As it was partly a performance class, performing excerpts from Shakespeare's works helped emphasize how the actors' performance is important for a scene.
